What is ASL?
American Sign Language — the language of the Deaf community
🤟
American Sign Language
ASL is a complete, complex language that uses signs made with the hands, combined with facial expressions and body posture. It is the primary language of many Deaf and hard-of-hearing people in the United States and Canada.
👥
The Deaf & HoH Community
Over 70 million Deaf people around the world use sign languages as their primary means of communication. The Deaf community has its own rich culture, history and traditions. Learning ASL is a gateway to understanding and connecting with this community.
🌍
Why Learn Sign Language?
Learning ASL breaks down communication barriers, promotes inclusion, and opens doors to new friendships. It benefits everyone — parents of Deaf children, healthcare workers, teachers, and anyone who wants to communicate across the hearing divide.
🧠
Cognitive Benefits
Research shows that learning a visual language like ASL improves spatial reasoning, memory and multitasking. It also creates new neural pathways and has been shown to help with early literacy development in children.
